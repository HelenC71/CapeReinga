Cape Reinga, situated at the northernmost tip of New Zealand's North Island, holds a rich and captivating history. It is steeped in ancient mythology and cultural significance. For centuries, Maori ancestors believed that Cape Reinga served as the departure point for the spirits of the deceased as they embarked on their final journey to the afterlife. The cape's Maori name, Te Rerenga Wairua, translates to "leaping-off place of spirits," further highlighting its spiritual importance.

Over time, Cape Reinga has become a cherished landmark, drawing visitors from around the world to witness its breathtaking beauty and to pay homage to its historical and cultural significance. Today, it stands as a symbol of connection to the past and a testament to the enduring Maori heritage that continues to thrive in New Zealand.
